## Design Context

### Users
This guide is for Carter's friends who are visiting Taipei for the first time. They need a fast, visual-first way to discover where to eat, drink, explore, and go out without having to decode a dense itinerary.

### Brand Personality
Fun, visually appealing, and helpful. The experience should feel welcoming, local, energetic, and confident enough to make first-time visitors feel excited instead of overwhelmed.

### Aesthetic Direction
Image-first and high-energy, with polished motion and strong visual hierarchy. The interface should feel memorable and a little celebratory, but still stay practical and scannable so people can make plans quickly.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with imagery so each place feels desirable before it feels informational.
2. Keep motion playful and cinematic, but never let it slow down browsing.
3. Make first-time visitors feel guided, not buried in options.
4. Favor confident, high-contrast hierarchy over cluttered decoration.
5. Treat this as a flagship-quality travel artifact, not a bare utility page.
